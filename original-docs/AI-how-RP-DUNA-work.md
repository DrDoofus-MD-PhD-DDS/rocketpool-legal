# **Internal Memo: Evaluating a DUNA for pDAO Committee Protection**

**Status:** Internal Working Document
**Audience:** pDAO Committee Members & Contributors
**Purpose:** Summarize recent discussions on using a DUNA to protect pDAO committees while minimizing cost, complexity, and risk.

---

## 1. Background and Scope

Rocket Pool’s protocol DAO (pDAO) currently operates without a legal entity. While the core development team is covered by Rocket Pool Pty Ltd (Australia), pDAO committees and contributors operate in a legal gray area that may expose individuals to personal liability under certain legal theories (e.g., general partnership concepts).

This memo focuses on **whether a DUNA (Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association)** could serve as a **lightweight, low-cost legal wrapper** for pDAO committees (e.g., GMC, IMC, RPIP Editors, Security Council), without attempting to wrap the entire pDAO or its treasury at this time.

---

## 2. Why a DUNA Is a Good Fit for Rocket Pool Committees

A DUNA aligns well with Rocket Pool’s current needs because it:

* Provides **limited liability protection** for people acting on behalf of committees
* Supports **role-based protection** (protection follows the committee seat, not the individual)
* Allows **pseudonymous or anonymous participation**
* Avoids forcing members to formally “join” a company or disclose identities
* Has **minimal setup and ongoing overhead**
* Fits a **non-commercial, governance-focused mandate**

Critically, a DUNA does **not** require:

* Ownership interests
* Equity
* Profit distribution
* Member registries filed with the state
* BOI/FinCEN reporting (under current rules)

This makes it materially easier to operate than DAO LLCs or offshore foundations.

---

## 3. Cost Expectations (Designed for a “Poor DAO”)

### One-Time Setup (Estimated)

* Draft charter / governing document (with legal review): **$1,000–$8,000**
* Registered agent (optional but recommended): **$100–$300 / year**

**Typical one-time total:**
👉 **$3,000–$5,000** (lean but defensible)

### Ongoing Annual Costs

* Required filings: **None**
* Registered agent: **$100–$300**
* Optional light legal or accounting review: **$500–$2,000**

**Lean annual baseline:**
👉 **$100–$500**
**Conservative annual budget:**
👉 **$1,500–$3,000**

Compared to other structures, this is extremely low.

---

## 4. Membership and Identity Requirements

A key advantage of a DUNA is **flexibility around membership**.

* Committee members **do not need to sign contracts** or file paperwork
* Membership can be defined by:

  * pDAO governance decisions
  * RPIPs
  * Internal records
* Members may remain:

  * Pseudonymous
  * Known only by Discord handle or wallet address

Typically, only:

* One organizer or agent (possibly a service provider or attorney)
  must be legally identified.

Protection flows from **acting within an authorized committee role**, not from formal enrollment.

---

## 5. Relationship to the pDAO

Under the proposed model:

* The **pDAO remains unincorporated**
* The **DUNA is not “the DAO”**
* The DUNA does **not** represent RPL holders
* The DUNA does **not** control protocol governance or tokenomics

Instead, the DUNA functions as a **service and execution layer**, authorized by pDAO governance to carry out specific committee-related activities (e.g., grants administration, incentives coordination, security response, indemnification).

This separation is intentional and important.

---

## 6. Tax Implications (High-Level, Practical View)

### Committee Funds (RPL Held in Safes)

* Holding RPL in a committee Safe is **not a taxable event**
* Receiving RPL from pDAO allocations is **not income by itself**
* Taxes arise only when:

  * RPL is sold or swapped
  * RPL is paid to a person or business

### DUNA Holding and Spending Funds

* A DUNA may hold and disburse RPL for programmatic purposes
* No tax is typically owed unless:

  * The DUNA generates net income
  * The DUNA engages in unrelated business activity

### Committee Stipends

* **Stipends paid to individuals are taxable income**
* This is already true today, with or without a DUNA
* Paid in RPL → income measured at fair market value at payment time

The DUNA does not eliminate personal tax obligations; it clarifies and formalizes them.

---

## 7. What the DUNA Should Do (Safe Activities)

To remain low-risk and low-cost, a DUNA should be limited to:

* Administering committee budgets
* Paying stipends and vendors
* Coordinating grants and incentives
* Executing pDAO-authorized actions
* Holding insurance
* Providing indemnification
* Supporting emergency/security actions

These activities align with a **non-pecuniary, nonprofit purpose**.

---

## 8. What the DUNA Should Not Do (Key Guardrails)

To avoid legal and tax complications, the DUNA should **not**:

* Originate or sponsor tokenomics changes
* Recommend increasing RPL-based yields (e.g., voter_share)
* Advocate RPL burns or value-enhancing mechanisms
* Receive ongoing fees or revenue from third-party protocols
* Engage in active trading or yield strategies
* Present itself as protocol leadership or ownership

Committee members may still participate in the pDAO **in their individual capacity**, but the DUNA itself should remain neutral on economic policy.

---

## 9. Asset Conversion (RPL → USDC)

* Converting RPL to USDC for **volatility management** is generally acceptable
* This introduces:

  * Accounting requirements
  * Potential realized gains/losses
* Should be:

  * Limited
  * Policy-based
  * Focused on stability, not profit

This is manageable but should be done sparingly.

---

## 10. Why We Are Not Wrapping the Entire pDAO (Yet)

At this stage:

* The pDAO treasury is not a legal entity
* Funds are allocated via onchain governance
* No profits or dividends are distributed
* Tax exposure arises downstream, not at the treasury level

Wrapping the entire pDAO would:

* Greatly increase cost and complexity
* Force difficult questions about representation and identity
* Go beyond what is currently needed

Focusing on committees first is a **pragmatic, incremental approach**.

---

## 11. Summary Assessment

A DUNA offers:

* Meaningful risk reduction
* Automatic protection for committee members
* Compatibility with pseudonymity
* Very low cost
* Minimal bureaucracy

If scoped carefully and conservatively, it appears to be **the best fit** among current options for protecting Rocket Pool’s governance contributors without compromising decentralization or imposing undue overhead.
