# Draft Charter Principles

This is not a charter. It is a list of principles that counsel could use to draft one.

## 1. Narrow purpose

The entity exists only to support pDAO-approved committee and administrative work.

It does not govern Rocket Pool, control the protocol, represent RPL holders, or manage the pDAO.

## 2. No equity or profit distributions

The entity has no equity owners. Members or covered participants do not receive profit distributions.

Reasonable compensation for actual work may be allowed if documented and approved under policy.

## 3. Role-based coverage

Covered participants should be defined by pDAO-recognized role where possible.

Coverage should apply only while acting in good faith within authorized scope.

## 4. Pseudonymity where practical

The entity should preserve pseudonymous participation where legally and practically possible.

This does not override legal, tax, banking, insurance, sanctions, or court requirements.

## 5. pDAO authority remains outside the entity

The pDAO retains governance authority. The entity is not a substitute for pDAO votes, RPIPs, Snapshot, onchain governance, or other approved governance processes.

## 6. Tokenomics firewall

The entity should not officially originate, sponsor, recommend, or advocate tokenomics changes designed primarily to increase RPL value or RPL staking rewards.

Covered people may still act in personal pDAO capacities if they separate those actions from official covered roles.

## 7. Third-party funds policy

Third-party funds may be accepted only if documented, restricted to approved purposes, not treated as profit, and not conditioned on governance outcomes.

## 8. Asset conversion policy

RPL may be converted to stable assets only for budgeting, payment, and volatility-management purposes, not speculation or trading.

## 9. Conflicts policy

Covered participants should disclose material conflicts where practical and avoid official decisions where a direct material conflict exists.

## 10. Records

The entity should keep records of roles, approvals, funds received, funds spent, conversions, stipends, vendor payments, contracts, insurance, and indemnification claims.
