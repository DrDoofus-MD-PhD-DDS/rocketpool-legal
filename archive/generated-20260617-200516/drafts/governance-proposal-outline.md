# Governance Proposal Outline: Legal Service Layer Review

This is a possible outline for a future pDAO proposal. It is not ready for use until counsel reviews the structure.

## Title

Authorize Scoped Legal Review for a Committee-First pDAO Service Layer

## Summary

This proposal authorizes a limited legal and tax review of a possible service-layer entity for pDAO committee and administrative work. The purpose is not to wrap the pDAO or create DAO leadership. The purpose is to evaluate whether a DUNA or similar low-overhead structure can help covered participants safely carry out pDAO-approved work.

## Motivation

The pDAO has committees and contributors performing useful work, but no legal service layer that can sign contracts, hold insurance, pay vendors, receive restricted funds, or provide clear indemnification.

The pDAO should explore a practical structure before asking volunteers to take unnecessary personal risk or forcing each contributor to create a personal LLC.

## Scope of review

Counsel should evaluate:

- DUNA vs alternative entity forms;
- role-based protection;
- pseudonymous participation;
- indemnification;
- insurance;
- tax treatment of RPL and stable assets;
- third-party incentive funds;
- BOI/CTA and other filing requirements;
- charter language;
- language that avoids implying central DAO management.

## Constraints

The reviewed structure must not:

- wrap the full pDAO;
- claim to represent all RPL holders;
- control tokenomics;
- manage the protocol;
- receive profits for distribution to members;
- sell governance outcomes.

## Deliverables

Counsel should produce:

- recommended entity form;
- setup steps;
- estimated setup and annual costs;
- required filings and responsible persons;
- sample purpose/scope language;
- indemnification framework;
- tax/accounting workflow;
- third-party funds policy;
- open risks and unresolved questions.
