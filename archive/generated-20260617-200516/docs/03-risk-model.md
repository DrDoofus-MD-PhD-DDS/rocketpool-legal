# Risk Model

This document explains the risks the service-layer project is meant to reduce. It should not be read as a prediction that the pDAO will be sued or that any particular person is liable.

## Main risk: unwrapped group activity

Unwrapped DAOs create uncertainty because courts and plaintiffs may try to characterize them as unincorporated associations, general partnerships, or other collective legal arrangements.

In some DAO cases, plaintiffs and regulators have argued that governance-token holders, voters, major backers, or active participants should be treated as members or partners of an unincorporated group. Those theories remain contested and fact-specific, but they are not hypothetical.

## Why committee work is different from passive voting

The risk is probably not evenly distributed across all pDAO activity.

Lower-risk activity may include ordinary passive governance participation, discussion, or voting. Higher-risk activity may include repeated, visible, operational work such as:

- approving grants;
- administering incentive programs;
- negotiating or interacting with third parties;
- signing or informally agreeing to terms;
- managing funds;
- executing transfers;
- making emergency protocol changes;
- paying contributors or vendors.

This is why a committee-first structure may be more practical than wrapping the entire pDAO.

## What a service layer can reduce

A service layer may reduce risk by:

- creating a legal party for contracts;
- allowing insurance to be purchased;
- providing indemnification for covered roles;
- documenting that committee members act within defined authority;
- separating committee execution from personal activity;
- reducing the need for individuals to sign personally or act as informal agents.

## What a service layer cannot solve

A service layer cannot eliminate all risk.

It may not protect actions outside scope. It may not prevent claims against individuals. It may not control how a court characterizes the pDAO. It may not solve securities, commodities, sanctions, tax, or consumer-protection issues. It may also create its own filing, tax, accounting, and governance obligations.

## Current public-source background

Public reporting and legal commentary have discussed DAO liability theories in cases involving Ooki DAO, bZx DAO, Compound DAO, MakerDAO, and Lido DAO. Some rulings allowed claims to proceed under unincorporated-association or partnership-style theories, but the law remains unsettled and fact-specific.

- Reuters Legal, “Digital assets and DAOs: new theories of liability” (June 10, 2024): https://www.reuters.com/legal/legalindustry/digital-assets-daos-new-theories-liability-2024-06-10/

## Working conclusion

The risk to solve first is not theoretical “all token holder risk.” The first practical risk is people doing repeated, visible, pDAO-approved operational work without a legal service layer.
