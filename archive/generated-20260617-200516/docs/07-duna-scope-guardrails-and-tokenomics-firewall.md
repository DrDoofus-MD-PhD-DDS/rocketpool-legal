# DUNA Scope, Guardrails, and Tokenomics Firewall

A service layer is useful only if it stays narrow. This document proposes guardrails to prevent the structure from becoming DAO management or a token-holder value vehicle.

## Core scope rule

The DUNA or service entity should exist to support approved work. It should not lead the pDAO.

Suggested rule:

> The service entity may carry out administrative, contractual, insurance, indemnification, payment, and execution tasks for pDAO-approved committee functions. It may not govern the Rocket Pool protocol, control protocol parameters except through specifically authorized emergency processes, or act as the representative body for RPL holders.

## Tokenomics firewall

The DUNA should not officially originate, sponsor, recommend, or advocate changes whose primary purpose is to increase RPL value or RPL staking returns.

Examples of activities the DUNA should avoid in its official capacity:

- increasing voter_share;
- advocating RPL burns;
- promoting RPL price appreciation;
- designing tokenomics changes for holder value;
- making official recommendations on RPL staking yield;
- treating RPL holders as equity-like owners of the service entity.

## Personal capacity is different

DUNA-covered people may still have personal roles in Rocket Pool.

A committee member may also be a node operator, RPL staker, delegate, RPIP author, voter, or forum participant. The key is hat separation.

A person may advocate a tokenomics RPIP in a personal capacity, but should not present that advocacy as an official DUNA action unless the DUNA's scope has been reviewed and authorized by counsel.

## RPIP Editors

RPIP Editors are a special case. Editors may process tokenomics RPIPs neutrally without becoming advocates for those proposals.

Neutral process work may include:

- formatting;
- checking template requirements;
- shepherding status changes;
- coordinating review timelines;
- confirming process requirements;
- publishing or merging approved process updates.

Editors should avoid using the official editor role to endorse tokenomics substance.

## Conflict-of-interest guardrails

The service layer should adopt basic conflict rules.

Examples:

- disclose material financial conflicts where practical;
- abstain from official decisions where the conflict is direct and material;
- separate personal advocacy from covered committee action;
- document decisions involving third-party funds;
- avoid accepting funds tied to specific governance outcomes.

## Nonprofit / non-profit-distribution guardrail

A DUNA may be nonprofit without being a charity. The key principle is that it should not distribute profits to members.

Reasonable compensation for actual work may be permissible, but should be documented as compensation, not profit-sharing.

## Counsel questions

- What exact language should define the DUNA's purpose?
- What activities would risk making the DUNA a manager, promoter, or issuer-like actor?
- How strict should the tokenomics firewall be?
- Can committee members receive stipends while also being RPL stakers or node operators?
- What conflict policy is sufficient without becoming too bureaucratic?
