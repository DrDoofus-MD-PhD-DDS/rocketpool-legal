# Research Note: AI-discussion-RPL-fee-DUNA.md Summary

The root-level file `AI-discussion-RPL-fee-DUNA.md` is useful for role separation and tokenomics-firewall analysis.

Main ideas to preserve:

- Committee members may also be node operators, RPL stakers, or pDAO voters.
- Those personal roles should not automatically disqualify someone from DUNA-covered work.
- The important distinction is official role vs personal capacity.
- The DUNA should not act officially to increase RPL value or RPL staking rewards.
- DUNA-covered people can still vote or advocate personally if they do not present that advocacy as an official DUNA position.
- RPIP Editors can process tokenomics-related RPIPs neutrally without endorsing the substance.

Changes made in the new docs:

- The tokenomics firewall was moved into `docs/07-duna-scope-guardrails-and-tokenomics-firewall.md`.
- The language guide now includes specific words to avoid.
- The internal memo now separates personal governance activity from covered official action.
