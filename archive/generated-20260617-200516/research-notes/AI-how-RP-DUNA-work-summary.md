# Research Note: AI-how-RP-DUNA-work.md Summary

The root-level file `AI-how-RP-DUNA-work.md` is useful as an operating-model note.

Main ideas to preserve:

- A DUNA could support pDAO committees without wrapping the whole pDAO.
- The pDAO should stay unincorporated for now.
- The DUNA should not be “the DAO” or represent all RPL holders.
- Membership or coverage might be based on governance-recognized roles.
- Pseudonymous members may be possible, but some organizer/agent/bank/tax person may still need to be identified.
- The DUNA could administer budgets, pay vendors, hold insurance, and indemnify covered people.
- RPL-to-USDC conversion should be for stability and payments, not trading.
- Ongoing third-party revenue is risky and needs careful limits.

Changes made in the new docs:

- Strong tax and filing claims were softened into counsel questions.
- Third-party funds were split into their own document.
- Role-based protection and pseudonymity were promoted into a core design doc.
- The DUNA was framed as a candidate structure, not a final conclusion.
