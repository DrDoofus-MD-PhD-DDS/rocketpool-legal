# Open Questions for Counsel

This is the consolidated checklist for legal and tax review.

## Entity choice

1. Is a DUNA the best fit for a single shared pDAO committee service layer?
2. What alternatives should be compared: nonprofit association, LLC, DAO LLC, nonprofit corporation, Swiss Association, Cayman foundation, or no entity?
3. Does a DUNA work if the pDAO itself remains unwrapped?
4. Does the DUNA risk being treated as the pDAO if it supports multiple committees?

## Scope and governance

5. What purpose language best defines the entity as a service layer, not DAO management?
6. Can the pDAO approve, fund, defund, or overturn committee work without making the DUNA a manager of the pDAO?
7. Should each committee have separate internal policies or accounts within one DUNA?
8. What emergency authority can the Security Council have without changing the analysis?

## Membership and pseudonymity

9. Can protected status be defined by pDAO election or appointment?
10. Must each protected person affirmatively consent to membership?
11. Can wallet addresses or handles be used for member/role records?
12. Which people must be legally identified to the state, IRS, bank, insurer, registered agent, or counsel?
13. Can pseudonymous members receive indemnification?
14. Can pseudonymous contributors be paid in a compliant way?

## Liability protection and indemnification

15. What liability protection does a DUNA actually provide under current law?
16. Does protection apply to members, managers, agents, volunteers, contractors, or committee participants?
17. What acts are outside scope?
18. What indemnification language should the pDAO and/or DUNA adopt?
19. What insurance is available and worth buying?

## RPL, rETH, and securities / commodities issues

20. What is the current risk that RPL is treated as a security in the relevant context?
21. What is the current risk that rETH or liquid staking activity is treated as securities activity?
22. Does the service layer holding, converting, or paying RPL change the analysis?
23. Should the DUNA avoid public statements about RPL value, yield, burns, or voter_share?
24. What role, if any, can the DUNA play in tokenomics process support?

## Tax and accounting

25. What is the tax treatment of pDAO treasury RPL inflation?
26. What is the tax treatment of pDAO-to-committee Safe transfers?
27. What is the tax treatment of pDAO-to-DUNA transfers?
28. What is the tax treatment when the DUNA converts RPL to USDC?
29. How should stipends paid in RPL be reported?
30. How should payments to non-U.S. contributors be handled?
31. What records should be kept for cost basis, FMV, and approvals?
32. What entity tax return, if any, would the DUNA file?
33. Should the DUNA seek any federal tax exemption, or remain simply nonprofit under state law?

## Third-party funds

34. Can the DUNA or IMC receive funds from third-party protocols?
35. Should third-party funds be limited to restricted/pass-through program funding?
36. What arrangements would look like revenue sharing, commissions, or selling governance influence?
37. Are there broker, money-transmission, sanctions, advertising, commodities, or securities concerns?
38. What disclosures should be made to the pDAO?

## BOI / CTA and filings

39. Does the current federal BOI/CTA reporting regime apply to the proposed entity?
40. Are domestic entities currently exempt, and could that change?
41. What state filings, registered agent requirements, annual reports, or organizer disclosures are required?
42. What bank/KYC requirements should be expected?

## Deliverable requested from counsel

The most useful counsel deliverable would be a practical memo answering:

- recommended entity form;
- minimum setup steps;
- expected annual cost and filings;
- pseudonymity limits;
- tax/accounting workflow;
- indemnification and insurance options;
- charter language;
- language to avoid centralization or token-value implications.
