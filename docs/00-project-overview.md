# Project Overview

## Purpose

Rocket Pool's pDAO currently relies on committees and contributors to perform real work: grants, incentives, governance-process administration, treasury execution, emergency response, and possible future coordination with vendors and third parties.

The pDAO itself is not a legal entity. That creates practical problems. The pDAO cannot easily sign contracts, hold insurance, open ordinary accounts, accept outside funding, pay vendors cleanly, or provide clear liability protection to people doing approved work.

This project explores whether a narrow legal service layer could help solve those problems without wrapping the entire pDAO.

## Preferred current framing

The preferred framing is **committee-first**.

The project is not trying to create a board, management company, officer group, or central controlling entity for Rocket Pool. It is trying to create a safer way for people to carry out work that the pDAO has already authorized.

Core message:

> We are not creating leadership. We are creating a safe way for people to do work.

## What is in scope

In scope:

- legal protection for committee members and administrative contributors;
- a possible shared DUNA or similar service entity;
- role-based coverage for elected or appointed pDAO roles;
- contracts, insurance, indemnification, vendor payments, and basic administration;
- treasury-support tasks, including budget custody and possible asset conversion for spending stability;
- guardrails that keep the service layer from becoming DAO management.

## What is out of scope for now

Out of scope for now:

- wrapping the entire pDAO;
- claiming to represent all RPL holders or all node operators;
- putting the pDAO treasury under a centralized legal entity;
- creating a token-holder board or management company;
- having the service entity originate or advocate tokenomics changes meant to increase RPL value or RPL staking rewards.

## Key uncertainty

This project depends on legal and tax questions that are unsettled or fact-specific. These docs therefore distinguish between working assumptions and counsel questions.

The most important counsel questions are:

1. Can a DUNA or similar U.S. entity provide meaningful role-based protection for pseudonymous committee members?
2. Can coverage follow pDAO election or appointment rather than individualized membership documents?
3. What tax treatment applies when RPL moves from pDAO treasury to a Safe, committee, DUNA, vendor, or contributor?
4. Can the service layer receive third-party incentive funds without becoming a revenue-generating business or conflicted protocol operator?
5. What language best avoids implying that committees manage or control the pDAO?
