# Current pDAO Functions and Possible Coverage

This document maps the current pDAO-related bodies and roles that may need a legal service layer. The goal is not to define them as managers of the DAO. The goal is to identify where people are doing work and may need practical protection.

## GMC: Grants Management Committee

The GMC receives RPL from the pDAO treasury by onchain vote and awards grants for work that helps the protocol.

Possible service-layer needs:

- vendor or grantee agreements;
- stipend administration;
- grant documentation;
- insurance or indemnification for good-faith grant decisions;
- administrative support.

Important guardrail: the pDAO can overturn GMC decisions. The GMC should be framed as an approved grants-administration body, not as management of the DAO.

## IMC: Incentives Management Committee

The IMC receives RPL from the pDAO treasury and provides incentives for rETH/RPL usage, liquidity, integrations, and related ecosystem goals.

Possible service-layer needs:

- incentive-program documentation;
- contracts or written terms with third-party protocols;
- custody and conversion of budget assets;
- accounting and reporting;
- conflict review.

Important guardrail: third-party payments or incentives are a special risk category. The IMC/DUNA should avoid looking like a revenue-generating business-development company or a party selling influence over the pDAO.

## RPIP Editors

RPIP Editors oversee the RPIP process but generally do not originate proposals in their official editor role.

Possible service-layer needs:

- process documentation;
- neutral administrative support;
- indemnification for good-faith process work;
- separation between editor role and personal governance activity.

Important guardrail: RPIP Editors may process tokenomics RPIPs neutrally, but should not be treated as officially endorsing those proposals by performing process work.

## pDAO Treasurer

The pDAO Treasurer executes approved transfers and related treasury tasks.

Possible service-layer needs:

- clear authorization records;
- indemnification for execution of approved actions;
- accounting support;
- signer-policy documentation.

Important guardrail: execution is not discretion. The Treasurer should be described as carrying out approved tasks, not managing the pDAO treasury.

## GMC Admin

The GMC Admin is paid in RPL for administrative support.

Possible service-layer needs:

- compensation documentation;
- payment records;
- role-based coverage for administrative acts;
- basic contractor paperwork if appropriate.

## Security Council

The Security Council is a trusted group, including core team members, that can make emergency parameter changes if something dangerous or buggy happens.

Possible service-layer needs:

- emergency-action documentation;
- indemnification for good-faith emergency response within scope;
- role clarity between core-company responsibilities and pDAO-appointed emergency responsibilities.

Important guardrail: the Security Council should be described as an emergency safety mechanism, not ordinary protocol management.

## Possible future administrative / coordination function

The pDAO may eventually need a function that coordinates business-like work such as vendors, marketing teams, roadmap preparation, contracts, and third-party incentives, while still requiring pDAO approval for major decisions.

Possible service-layer needs:

- vendor contracts;
- payment administration;
- budget tracking;
- insurance;
- conflict review;
- scope limits.

Important guardrail: coordination is not leadership. This function should help execute pDAO-approved work, not become the pDAO's management layer.
