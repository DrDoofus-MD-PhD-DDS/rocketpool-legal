# DUNA Membership and Pseudonymity

This document focuses on one of the most important design goals: protection should follow the role, not the person.

## Design goal

The pDAO should avoid a system where every committee member must form a personal LLC, publicly dox themselves, or sign individualized legal membership documents before doing volunteer or low-paid work.

The preferred model is role-based coverage.

Possible rule:

> A covered participant is any person or wallet/handle holder who is duly elected, appointed, or authorized to perform a covered pDAO committee or administrative role under approved pDAO governance processes, while acting within the scope of that role.

## Why role-based coverage matters

Rocket Pool has pseudonymous contributors and rotating volunteer roles. A legal structure that only works for doxxed, individually enrolled members may be culturally and practically unusable.

Role-based protection would let a contributor know: if you are serving in a recognized role and acting within scope, the service layer is intended to protect and indemnify you.

## What records may be needed

Even if ordinary committee members remain pseudonymous, the service layer will likely need some records.

Possible records:

- governance vote or appointment record;
- wallet address or handle associated with the role;
- term dates;
- committee scope;
- conflict disclosures, possibly pseudonymous unless counsel requires more;
- transaction approvals;
- offboarding records.

## Identified organizer or agent

A realistic pseudonymity model may still require at least one identified organizer, registered agent, tax responsible person, bank/account signer, or counsel-facing representative.

The goal should not be “nobody is ever identified.” The goal should be that ordinary committee members are not forced to publicly dox solely to receive basic protection for approved work.

## Limits of pseudonymity

Pseudonymity may be limited by:

- banking requirements;
- insurance underwriting;
- tax reporting;
- sanctions screening;
- vendor KYC;
- subpoena or litigation discovery;
- compensation reporting;
- entity formation rules.

The docs should not promise anonymity. They should say the project seeks to preserve pseudonymous participation where legally and practically possible.

## Suggested language

Use:

- “covered participant”;
- “covered role”;
- “pDAO-recognized role”;
- “acting within authorized scope”;
- “role-based protection.”

Avoid:

- “secret members”;
- “anonymous directors”;
- “hidden managers”;
- “legal shield for all token holders.”

## Counsel questions

- Can a DUNA define membership or protected status by reference to pDAO governance records?
- Does each protected person need to consent to membership?
- Can a wallet address or pseudonymous handle be enough for internal role records?
- Which people must be identified to the state, IRS, bank, insurer, registered agent, or counsel?
- Can compensation be paid to pseudonymous contributors without creating unacceptable tax or compliance problems?
