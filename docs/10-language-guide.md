# Language Guide

Words matter. The goal is to describe the structure accurately without implying that committees are managers, directors, officers, or controlling members of the DAO.

## Core message

> We are not creating leadership. We are creating a safe way for people to do work.

## Preferred terms

Use terms like:

- service layer;
- execution layer;
- administrative support;
- committee support entity;
- legal service layer;
- covered participant;
- covered role;
- pDAO-approved work;
- pDAO-recognized role;
- role-based protection;
- neutral process support;
- good-faith work within scope;
- pDAO retains governance authority.

## Terms to avoid

Avoid terms like:

- board of the DAO;
- DAO management;
- directors of the pDAO;
- officers of the pDAO;
- controlling members;
- representatives of all RPL holders;
- management company;
- token-holder board;
- maximizing RPL value;
- shareholder value;
- protocol leadership;
- DUNA controls the pDAO;
- DUNA represents Rocket Pool.

## Better phrasing examples

Instead of:

> The DUNA manages the pDAO treasury.

Use:

> The service layer may help execute pDAO-approved treasury operations within a defined scope.

Instead of:

> The committee decides protocol strategy.

Use:

> The committee may coordinate approved workstreams and bring major decisions back to the pDAO.

Instead of:

> The DUNA represents RPL holders.

Use:

> The service layer supports covered contributors and committees; it does not represent RPL holders as a class.

Instead of:

> Editors approve tokenomics proposals.

Use:

> RPIP Editors administer the proposal process neutrally and do not endorse proposal substance in their editor role.

Instead of:

> The DUNA increases RPL value by improving tokenomics.

Use:

> Tokenomics advocacy should remain outside the official role of the service layer.

## Public communications tone

The tone should be practical, not scary.

Do not frame this as “we are all about to be sued.” Frame it as ordinary operational hygiene:

- people doing approved work deserve protection;
- vendors need a contracting party;
- insurance needs an insured entity;
- payment records should be clean;
- the pDAO should not force volunteers to take unnecessary personal risk.
