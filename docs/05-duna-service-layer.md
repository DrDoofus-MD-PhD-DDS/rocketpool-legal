# DUNA Service Layer

This document describes how a DUNA or similar entity might work as a narrow service layer for pDAO-approved committee work.

## Basic concept

The DUNA would not be the pDAO. It would not control Rocket Pool. It would not manage tokenomics. It would not represent all RPL holders.

It would be a legal service layer for defined work.

Possible purpose statement:

> The organization exists to provide administrative, contractual, insurance, indemnification, treasury-support, and execution services for pDAO-approved Rocket Pool committee and contributor functions. It does not govern the Rocket Pool protocol, represent RPL holders as a class, control protocol tokenomics, or act as the management body of the pDAO.

## Relationship to the pDAO

The pDAO remains the governance layer.

The service layer may support execution of actions that the pDAO has approved or that pDAO-approved committee rules authorize. The pDAO should retain ultimate governance authority, including the ability to approve, reject, amend, fund, defund, or overturn committee activity under existing governance rules.

## Possible covered functions

The service layer could support:

- GMC grants administration;
- IMC incentive administration;
- RPIP process support;
- pDAO Treasurer execution tasks;
- GMC Admin work;
- Security Council emergency functions;
- future pDAO-approved administrative or coordination work.

## Practical tools the service layer could provide

Subject to counsel review, the service layer might:

- sign vendor contracts;
- hold insurance;
- administer budgets;
- pay stipends and vendors;
- hold RPL or stable assets for approved spending;
- convert RPL to USDC or similar assets for budgeting stability;
- accept restricted/pass-through funds for approved incentive programs;
- indemnify covered participants for good-faith acts within scope;
- maintain records of roles, approvals, and transactions.

## What the service layer should not do

The service layer should not:

- claim to be Rocket Pool governance;
- direct the pDAO;
- represent all RPL holders;
- control the pDAO treasury;
- originate or advocate tokenomics changes in its official capacity;
- operate as a profit-making business;
- distribute profits to members;
- sell governance influence or access.

## Why one shared structure may be better than many

Separate legal entities for each committee could reduce cross-contamination, but that likely creates too much overhead. Rocket Pool has limited volunteer capacity and limited pDAO budget.

A single shared service layer is more practical if it can maintain internal role separation through policies, accounting categories, and scope limits.

## Counsel questions

- Can a DUNA provide liability protection to people acting in pDAO-recognized committee roles?
- Can membership or coverage be defined by governance election/appointment rather than signed paperwork?
- What minimum identified organizer, agent, or responsible person is required?
- What filings, tax returns, EIN, registered agent, banking, accounting, and insurance are required?
- Can the DUNA hold and convert RPL for operations?
- Can it receive third-party funds for incentive programs?
